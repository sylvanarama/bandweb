Press Kit
The Hounds of Cuchulain are a high-energy folk band from Victoria BC who perform a wide selection of Irish, Scottish, English, and original folk songs in the Celtic tradition: rowdy pub songs, tender ballads, spirited dance tunes, and ancient airs played with an authenticity and energy which brings the tradition to life for any occasion.

The group is led by seasoned performer and composer Wolf Edwards on lead vocal and guitar; and joined by long running musical partner Devo Bernard on the bass guitar; the multi-talented Madeleine Townley on vocals, Irish harp, and tin whistle; Dexter Simpson on Irish lead tenor banjo, and by the virtuoso fiddler and master of harmony Briana Kennedy.

Combining powerful vocals, driving guitar, classic irish banjo, inventive harmonies on the fiddle, sprightly leads on the whistle, as well as bold and bright accompaniment on the irish lever harp, the Hounds bring passion and life to the tradition of celtic music, creating a warm and inviting space for anyone to feel at home in, to sing along and stamp their feet to - true to the tradition of folk!

The Hounds of Cuchulain have reached their two year anniversary! In that short time they’ve performed over 150 shows at major folk festivals, theaters, pubs, community events, private functions and more. The band has released two full length albums, and head back to the studio this upcoming year to record an album of original songs.

The Hounds of Cuchulain are a high-energy folk band from Victoria BC who perform a wide selection of Irish, Scottish, English, and original folk songs in the Celtic tradition: rowdy pub songs, tender ballads, spirited dance tunes, and ancient airs played with an authenticity and energy which brings the tradition to life for any occasion.

The group is led by seasoned performer and composer Wolf Edwards on lead vocal and guitar; and joined by long running musical partner Devo Bernard on the bass guitar; the multi-talented Madeleine Townley on vocals, Irish harp, and tin whistle; Dexter Simpson on Irish lead tenor banjo, the virtuoso fiddler and master of harmony Briana Kennedy, and occasionally joined by Ben Garrison on mandolin.

Combining powerful vocals, driving guitar, classic irish banjo, inventive harmonies on the fiddle, sprightly leads on the whistle, as well as bold and bright accompaniment on the irish lever harp, the Hounds bring passion and life to the tradition of celtic music, creating a warm and inviting space for anyone to feel at home in, to sing along and stamp their feet to - true to the tradition of folk!

The group’s repertoire features songs from the 1600’s all the way up to the 1980s, arranged in a clear and energetic style reminiscent of such folk giants as the Dubliners, Pogues, and Corries. In addition to this, they bring in elements from the youthful urban revival of traditional music in the 60’s and 70’s and also from the humble, stripped down presentation such as can be found on many of the more traditional singer/songwriter albums of the 1960’s.

Traditional music enthusiasts will appreciate the song-tales of lost love and betrayal, the misdeeds of ramblers and rakes, emigration, hard drinking and hard living, sea shanties and worker’s songs. In addition to these ballads and chorus songs, the Hounds of Cuchulain also add the explosive musical energy of traditional instrumental jigs and reels, and original compositions in the celtic style by lead singer Wolf Edwards - focusing on local history, such as the tragic death of labour activist Albert “Ginger” Goodwin.

The Hounds of Cuchulain have reached their two year anniversary! In that short time they’ve performed over 150 shows: from pubs to private parties, on boats and at benefits, folk clubs to major folk festivals. The band has released two full length albums -“Rebels, Rogues, & Roustabouts” and “Here’s the Grog” - and are headed back to the studio this upcoming year to record an album of original songs.
